Privacy and HIPAA information

This section helps prospective clients understand how to evaluate BuddyBoard for environments that may involve protected health information.

Important: avoid stating that BuddyBoard is “HIPAA compliant” unless you have completed a legal, technical, and operational review for the exact deployment environment and can provide the supporting documentation.
What we can state today

BuddyBoard includes role-based access patterns, authentication, urgent communications, administrative controls, and data export features designed for operational use in therapy and school environments.

What should be reviewed before using for PHI

Formal HIPAA readiness depends on implementation details outside the app UI alone, including hosting, encryption configuration, access policies, staff procedures, logging, vendor agreements, and signed documentation.

Best practice for client conversations

Present this page as an information hub and pair it with a security packet, BAA workflow, privacy policy, and deployment-specific controls checklist.

Questions a buyer will ask

  • How is access restricted by role and organization?
  • How are user accounts authenticated and managed?
  • Where is data stored, and how is it backed up?
  • What logging exists for sensitive actions and exports?
  • Can the vendor sign a Business Associate Agreement?
  • What is the process for incident handling and notification?
  • What data can be exported for continuity or offboarding?

Documents to make available

  • Business Associate Agreement (BAA) availability
  • Security overview and architecture summary
  • Access control and role-permission model
  • Authentication and account protection details
  • Data storage, backup, and retention approach
  • Incident response and breach-notification process
  • Hosting and infrastructure information
  • Vendor and subprocessors list
  • Audit logging and export capabilities
  • Policies for onboarding, offboarding, and account review
